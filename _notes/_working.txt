%%"Manx Ballads & Music," (1896) edited by A. W. Moore, has these translated
%%verses which may have formed the basis for Barrand and Roberts' version.
%%endtext
%%vskip 0.8cm

W: We'll away to the wood, says Robin to Bobbin; 
W: We'll away to the wood, says Richard to Robin. 
W: We'll away to the wood, says Jack of the Land; 
W: We'll away to the wood, says everyone. 
W: 
W: What shall we do there? says Robin to Bobbin; 
W: Repeat these lines as above. 
W: 
W: We will hunt the wren, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: Repeat, etc.
W: 
W: Where is he? where is he? says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: In yonder green bush, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: I see him, I see him, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: How shall we get him down, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: With sticks and stones, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: He is dead, he is dead, says Robin to Bobbin., 
W: 
W: How shall we get him home? says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: We'll hire a cart, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: Whose cart shall we hire? says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: Johnny Bill Fell's, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: Who will stand driver? says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: Filley the Tweet, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: He's home, he's home, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: How shall we get him boil'd? says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: In the brewery pan, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: How shall we get him in? says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: With iron bars and a rope, says Robin to Bobbin 
W: 
W: He is in, he is in, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: He is boil'd, he is boil'd, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: How shall we get him out? says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: With a long pitchfork, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: He is out, he is out, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: Who's to dine at the dinner? says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: The King and the Queen, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: How shall we get him eat? says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: With knives and forks, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: He is eat, he is eat, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: 
W: The eyes for the blind, says Robin to Bobbin. 
W: The legs for the lame, says Richard to Robin; 
W: The pluck for the poor, says Jack of the land; 
W: The bones for the dogs, says every one. 
W: 
W: The wren, the wren, the king of all birds, 
W: We have caught, St. Stephen's Day, in the furze; 
W: Although he is little, his family's great, 
W: I pray you, good dame, do give us a treat.
